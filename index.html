Hiring Sequence
4 phases from current state to 50M ARR end state
Phase 1 — Now
~31M ARR · Immediate
👁 DO FIRST
· Audit HubSpot — data quality, ownership gaps, integration health
· Baseline analytics report — what's tracked, what's missing, what Spokes need
· Document AM programme current state — what exists vs. what's ad hoc
Analytics Lead
Everything data-related is blocked without this. Spokes have no analytical support and current capacity can't carry it.
→ Data & Analytics Pod
Systems & CRM Specialist
Frees Ops Lead from HubSpot admin so they can manage and govern. Without this, the Ops Lead role is just CRM admin.
→ Systems & CRM Pod
Customer Ops Strategist
No one owns AM programme design today. Expansion revenue has no operational backbone. Highest business impact hire.
→ Customer Ops Spoke
Phase 2 — Near Term
~33–37M ARR · H2 2026
👁 DO FIRST
· Zendesk Specialist: formalise weekly report into Core Ops (volume, SLA, top issues)
· Marketing attribution baseline via Dreamdata — know what's measured before hiring
· Revenue pipeline hygiene audit — understand CRM gaps before hiring analysts
Marketing Ops Lead
13-person team doing paid, events + organic needs a dedicated senior lead. Owns HubSpot marketing hub, attribution, and the Marketing→Sales handoff.
→ Marketing Ops Spoke
Customer Ops Owner
Strategist defaults to day-to-day without execution support. Hire immediately after Strategist is onboarded.
→ Customer Ops Spoke
Finance Ops Manager
Junior analyst can't operate at peer level with Senior Finance Manager. Mid-senior bridge needed before finance complexity scales.
→ Finance Ops Pod
Revenue Analyst I + Data Analyst I
Pipeline ops/SDR support and analytics delivery both need dedicated resource. Data Analyst I could be an internal movement.
→ Revenue Ops + Data Pod
Marketing Analyst I
Paid + attribution first — highest measurable ROI. Hire once Marketing Ops Lead has set the agenda.
→ Marketing Ops Spoke
Phase 3 — Scale
~38–45M ARR · H1 2027
👁 DO FIRST
· Review 90 days of Zendesk data — scope CS Ops as process builder vs. analyst based on what you find
· CRM hygiene audit — quantify the data quality problem before Rev Analyst II
· Automation backlog review — confirm AI Lead is blocked before hiring Analyst
Automation Analyst
AI Lead gets pulled everywhere. Analyst owns execution and maintenance so Lead stays on architecture and new builds.
→ AI & Automation Pod
Revenue Analyst II
CRM hygiene is its own workload at 15+ AEs and 15+ SDRs. Can't be combined with pipeline ops — split the role.
→ Revenue Ops Spoke
Marketing Analyst II
Events + organic is a separate motion to paid. One analyst is too thin for a 13-person team running all three channels.
→ Marketing Ops Spoke
CS Ops
Shape of this hire determined by Zendesk data. Process builder if workflows are broken; analyst if volume and SLA visibility is the problem.
→ Customer Ops Spoke
Phase 4 — Final Push
~45–50M ARR · H2 2027
👁 DO FIRST
· Review analytics demand across all Spokes — confirm Data Analyst II is needed vs. redistributing
· Map full systems architecture (HubSpot + warehouse + product) before scoping RevOps Architect
Data Analyst II
Analytics demand from three Spokes plus Hub work outgrows one analyst as reporting complexity grows toward 50M.
→ Data & Analytics Pod
RevOps / Biz Systems Architect
HubSpot + warehouse + product data integration needs a senior architect at this scale. Not needed until systems are deeply interconnected.
→ Hub · Systems or standalone
Watch items
· Customer Ops Analyst if Strategist gets pulled into execution
· Second CS Ops if customer base grows significantly
· Marketing Analyst III if ABM launches
· Zendesk solid-line if dotted governance breaks down
